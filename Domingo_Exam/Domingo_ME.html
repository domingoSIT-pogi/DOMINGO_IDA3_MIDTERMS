Sisig tofu

Introduction: Sisig is a beloved Filipino dish known for its savory, tangy, and slightly spicy flavors, traditionally made with pork.

This sisig tofu version offers a plant-based alternative that's just as delicious and satisfying.

Originating from the culinary traditions of the Philippines, sisig has evolved over time, and this tofu adaptation makes it accessible for vegans and vegetarians.

In this recipe, we'll use firm tofu to mimic the texture of the original, combined with fresh ingredients to create a mouthwatering dish perfect for sharing with friends and family.

Ingredients

Ingredient Quantity Notes
Firm tofu 1 block (about 14 oz) Pressed and cubed
Onion 1 medium, finely chopped Red or white
Calamansi juice or lemon juice 2 tablespoons For tanginess
Soy sauce 2 tablespoons Low-sodium preferred
Green chilies 2-3, sliced Adjust for spice level
Garlic 3 cloves, minced Fresh for best flavor
Vegetable oil 2 tablespoons For frying
Salt and pepper To taste Season generously

Recipe Steps

Press the tofu to remove excess water. Cut into small cubes and set aside.

Heat vegetable oil in a large pan over medium-high heat. Add the cubed tofu and fry until golden and crispy, about 10-15 minutes. Remove and set aside.

In the same pan, sauté garlic and onion until fragrant, about 2-3 minutes.

Add the green chilies and cook for another minute.

Stir in soy sauce, calamansi juice, salt, and pepper. Mix well.

Add the crispy tofu back into the pan and toss to coat evenly. Cook for 2-3 more minutes to absorb flavors.

Serve hot on a sizzling plate if possible, garnished with additional chilies.
