Nike, founded in 1964 as Blue Ribbon Sports and rebranded in 1971, has become a global leader in athletic footwear, apparel, and equipment. Known for its innovative designs and cutting-edge technology, Nike revolutionized the sportswear industry by blending performance with style. The brand’s iconic “Just Do It” slogan and high-profile athlete collaborations, including with Michael Jordan and Serena Williams, have cemented its status as a cultural powerhouse. Nike continues to inspire athletes of all levels with its focus on empowerment, excellence, and pushing the boundaries of both sport and fashion.

Carhartt, founded in 1889 in Michigan, has long been a symbol of durability and practicality in workwear. Originally designed for laborers, farmers, and outdoor workers, the brand became known for its tough, high-quality fabrics like duck canvas and denim, built to withstand the harshest conditions. Over the years, Carhartt expanded its appeal beyond the workplace, especially through its Carhartt WIP (Work In Progress) line, which combines the brand’s rugged legacy with a streetwear aesthetic. This blend of utility and style has made Carhartt a staple in both blue-collar and fashion-forward circles, maintaining its commitment to craftsmanship while embracing contemporary trends.
